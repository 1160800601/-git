Methodology
Interviews were conducted October 27-30, 2019 among a random national sample of 1,040 registered voters (RV). Landline (229) and cellphone (811) telephone numbers were randomly selected for inclusion in the survey using a probability proportionate to size method, which means phone numbers for each state are proportional to the number of voters in each state. Results based on the full sample have a margin of sampling error of ± 3 percentage points. The Fox News Poll is conducted under the joint direction of Beacon Research (D) (formerly known as Anderson Robbins Research) and Shaw & Company Research (R).
 Note: In 2019, the firm Anderson Robbins Research changed its name to Beacon Research; the Fox News bipartisan polling team remains unchanged.
Fieldwork conducted by Braun Research, Inc. of Princeton, NJ. Fox News Polls before 2011 were conducted by Opinion Dynamics Corporation.
 Results are of registered voters, unless otherwise noted.  An asterisk (*) is used for percentages of less than one-half percent.  A dash (-) represents a value of zero. Some percentages may not add to 100% due to rounding. In the same way, percentages in “total” columns may be one point more or less than the sum of their parts due to rounding.
